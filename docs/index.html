How it works & limits

Everything happens on your computer.

tagit runs entirely in this browser tab — there's no server behind it, no account, no analytics, no tracking. Your photos are never uploaded anywhere, and nothing about them or you is collected. The only network requests it ever makes are ones you explicitly ask for: loading map tiles to show a photo's location, or talking to iNaturalist if you paste in your own API token. Close this tab and nothing is sent or kept, aside from a few small preferences — your chosen taxonomy, theme, watermark settings — saved locally in this browser (never anywhere else).

How it works

  • Captions are written into the three fields Apple Photos reads: IPTC Caption-Abstract, XMP dc:description, EXIF ImageDescription.
  • Save → _labelled; Skip → _skipped; Can’t determine → _undetermined (“Indet.”); Delete → _deleted.
  • Full folder editing works in Chrome/Edge/Brave/Arc. Safari/Firefox: drag in and download copies.
  • This build handles JPEG only — RAW/HEIC need the desktop version.
  • iNaturalist: paste a token in the iNaturalist tab, then click Identify with iNaturalist to get AI suggestions (with confidence %) for the current photo (uses its GPS & date).
  • Map: photos with GPS get a Show map control in the photo info; it appears as a small preview in the photo's bottom-right corner — click it to enlarge.
  • Keys: ← → navigate · 1–9 pick a suggestion · Delete discard (only with nothing selected). Just start typing a letter anywhere to jump into the search box — Rotate/Undo/Can’t-determine/Same-as-previous are one click away as buttons instead of shortcut letters, so a letter key always means “start a search.”
  • In the search box, type an abbreviation of each word — e.g. dro rot for Drosera rotundifolia, dact fuch for Dactylorhiza maculata subsp. fuchsii — then press Enter to save that match and move to the next photo in one step. Clicking a suggestion/recently-used chip instead just adds it to the current determination without saving.
